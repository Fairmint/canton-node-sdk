# Understanding Shares

*This article is for founders and admins using Fairmint Studio.*

Shares represent direct equity ownership in your company. In Fairmint, a **Shares** series is used when you want to issue stock — typically in a priced round (e.g., Series A) or when your legal agreement grants the right to receive or purchase shares (e.g., a stock option agreement or an equity warrant).

## When to Use Shares

Use a Shares series when:

- You are conducting a **priced equity round** (Series A, Series B, etc.)
- Your legal agreement is for **direct stock** rather than a convertible instrument
- You are issuing shares under a **stock option agreement** or **equity warrant**
- You have a **pre-existing cap table** with shareholders you want to record in Fairmint

If your agreement converts into equity at a later date (e.g., a SAFE or convertible note), use a [SAFE series](2.2-create-fundraise-safe.md) instead.

## How Shares Differ from Other Instrument Types

| Feature | Shares | SAFEs | Warrants | Grants |
|---------|--------|-------|----------|--------|
| Equity type | Direct ownership | Converts to equity later | Right to purchase shares later | Compensation-based equity |
| Typical use | Priced rounds | Pre-seed / seed fundraising | Alongside SAFEs or notes | Employee / advisor compensation |
| Share price | Set at creation | N/A (valuation cap / discount) | N/A | N/A |
| Created via | + New > Raise capital | + New > Raise capital | + New > Raise capital | + New > Grant equity |

## Creating a Shares Series

Shares are created through the **Raise capital** flow:

1. Click **+ New** in the left menu
2. Select **Raise capital**
3. In the series creation form, under **Type of financial instrument**, select **Share**
4. A **Share Price** field appears — enter the price per share for your round
5. Complete the remaining fields (series name, amount to raise, solicitation type, legal agreement, pitch deck)
6. Click **Save and continue**

For a full walkthrough of the creation form, see [Create a Fundraise (Roll Up)](2.1-create-fundraise-rollup.md).

## Managing a Shares Series

Once created, the Shares series appears under the **Shares** section in the left menu. Clicking it opens the Series Detail page with the same tabs and features as any fundraising series:

- **Stakeholders** — view shareholders and their holdings
- **Investments** — track individual share purchases
- **Invitation Links** — create and manage links for investors
- **Activity** — see a timeline of events
- **Compliance** — KYC/AML status of investors
- **Terms** — view and edit deal terms, including the share price

For details on managing your series, see [Manage Your Series](2.11-manage-series-settings.md).

## Where to Find Shares

- **Left menu**: Under the **Shares** section (appears once you create a Shares series)
- **Cap Table**: Shares show up in [Summary View](3.4-cap-table-summary-view.md) under "Summary Capitalization"
- **Dashboard**: Active Shares series appear in the Fundraising section of your [Dashboard](1.6-dashboard-overview.md)
