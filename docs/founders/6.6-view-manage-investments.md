# View and Manage Investments

*This article is for founders and admins using Fairmint Studio.*

The Investments page shows all investments across your fundraising series. Click **Investments** in the left menu to open this page.

## Investments List

### Series Tabs

At the top, tabs let you filter by series:

- **All** — every investment across all fundraising series
- One tab per fundraising series (e.g., "Seed Round", "Series A") — filters to that series only

Switching tabs resets the search and filter.

### Table Columns

| Column | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **DATE** | Date and time the investment was created |
| **NAME** | Investor's name with avatar |
| **VALUE** | Dollar amount and the series name (e.g., "$9,000 in Aries") |
| **METHOD** | Payment method used (e.g., "Bank transfer" with reference code). Blank if the investor hasn't sent funds yet |
| **STATUS** | Current investment status (e.g., "Signed", "Check bank", "Executed") |

Click any row (or the arrow on the right) to open that investment's detail page. Investments that need your attention are highlighted with a **yellow left border**.

### Search, Filter & Pagination

- **Search by name or email** — text search field
- **Filter by** — dropdown to narrow by status (e.g., All investments, Signed, Funds sent, Executed, Canceled)
- **Pagination** — choose how many rows to show per page: 25, 50, 100, 250, or 500

### Export

Click **Export Investments** at the top of the page to download all investment data as a CSV file. On smaller screens, the button is labeled **Export**. The downloaded file is named after your company and today's date (e.g., "Acme-Investments-2026-03-30.csv").

The CSV includes these columns:

| Column | Example |
|--------|---------|
| **Investment Date** | 2026-03-23 |
| **Round Name** | Seed Round |
| **Investor Name** | Jane Smith |
| **Address** | 123 Main St, New York, 10001 |
| **Email Address** | jane@example.com |
| **Country Code** | US |
| **State Code** | NY |
| **Amount Invested** | 25000 |
| **Funds Received** | Yes or No |
| **Investment Method** | bank transfer (blank if not yet specified) |

## Investment Statuses

Each investment shows a colored status label. Here's what each one means and what to do:

| Status | Color | What It Means | What To Do |
|--------|-------|---------------|------------|
| **Pending signature** | Yellow | The investor hasn't signed the agreement yet | Wait for the investor to sign |
| **Signed** | Blue | The investor signed the agreement but hasn't sent funds yet | Wait for funds, or click "We received the funds" if you already have them |
| **Committed** | Blue | The investor has committed to invest | Wait for the investor to proceed |
| **Awaiting signature** | Yellow | The investor signed, but you need to countersign | Open the investment and sign the agreement |
| **Check bank** | Yellow | The investor initiated a bank transfer | Check your bank account, then confirm receipt with the date and "Confirm Investment" button |
| **Processing** | Blue | A USDC payment is being processed | Wait for the transfer to complete |
| **Check Wallet** | Yellow | The investor sent funds via Ethereum, Solana, or Canton | Verify the funds arrived in your wallet, then confirm |
| **Funds received** | Green | You confirmed receiving funds; shares/tokens still need to be issued | Click **"Confirm Investment"** to finalize |
| **Executed** | Green | Investment is complete — funds received and shares/tokens issued (or no issuance needed) | No action needed |
| **Issuing** | Blue | Shares/tokens are being issued — this usually takes a few moments | Wait for issuance to finish |
| **Declined** | Red | You declined this investment | No action needed |
| **Canceled** | Grey | The investment was canceled | No action needed. Note: investors cannot cancel once funds have been received or shares/tokens have been issued |
| **Transfer in** | Blue | This investment was transferred in from another holder | No action needed |
| **Transfer out** | Grey | This investment was transferred out to another holder | No action needed |

## Investment Detail Page

Click any investment row to see its full detail page.

### Header

At the top you'll see:

- The investor's **photo** and **name** (click the link icon to go to their full profile)
- Their **country flag**
- A **"Known investor"** label if they've been verified

### Investment Details

This card shows whether the person is investing as an **individual** or **on behalf of a company**, along with:

| Field | What You'll See |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Investment amount** | The dollar amount (e.g., "$9,000.00"). Once tokens are issued, it also shows the number of tokens |
| **Started on** | When the investment was created (e.g., "23 Mar 2026, at 07:09:31 AM CDT") |
| **Number of shares** | How many shares the investor is purchasing (only shown for priced rounds) |
| **Purchase agreement** | A link to download the signed PDF agreement. Shows "Pending signature" if not yet signed |
| **Fundraising round** | Which series this investment belongs to (e.g., "Aries") |
| **Funding method** | How the investor is paying (e.g., "Bank transfer"). If the investor hasn't sent funds yet, you'll see a message like "The investor hasn't yet sent the funds for this investment." You can also set the method manually using the edit icon |
| **Token warrant** | The token warrant purchase price (only shown if the round includes a token warrant) |
| **Total to transfer** | The full amount the investor needs to send, including the investment, warrant, and any processing fees |
| **Processing fees** | Any fees charged for this investment |
| **Reference code** | The bank transfer reference code the investor should include (only shown for bank transfers). Click the copy icon to copy it |
| **Transaction(s)** | A link to view the blockchain transaction (only shown for USDC or crypto payments) |
| **Invitation link used** | The URL the investor used to access your deal |
| **Token issuance** | The wallet address where tokens will be (or were) sent. Click to view it on the blockchain |

If this investment came from an exercised equity grant, you'll see a green banner linking to the original grant.

### Contact Information

Shows the investor's address and email. For company investments, both a **company contact card** and a **signer contact card** are displayed side by side.

### Status

On the right side you'll see a **Status** card that tells you where this investment stands and what to do next. It always shows:

- The current **status** (e.g., "Signed", "Check bank")
- When it was last **updated**
- A description of what's happening and your next step

Here's what you'll see depending on the status:

| Status | What You'll See |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Signed** | The investor signed but hasn't sent funds. You'll see a **"We received the funds"** link if you've already received payment outside the platform |
| **Check bank** | The investor initiated a bank transfer. Enter the **date you received the funds**, check the confirmation box, and click **"Confirm Investment"** |
| **Check Wallet** | Same as Check bank, but for Ethereum, Solana, or Canton payments — verify your wallet and confirm |
| **Awaiting signature** | You need to countersign the agreement. Click to sign |
| **Funds received** | Funds confirmed. If your round issues shares/tokens, click **"Confirm Investment"** to issue them |
| **Issuing** | Shares/tokens are being issued. This usually takes a few moments — you'll be notified when it's done |
| **Executed** | The investment is complete. Nothing to do |
| **Canceled** | The investor canceled this investment |
| **Declined** | You declined this investment |

If the agreement requires multiple signers, you'll see additional signature cards below — one for each remaining signer.

### Multiple Signers

Some deals require more than one person to sign the purchase agreement — for example, a company officer may need to countersign after the investor signs. When this applies:

- You'll see a **separate signature card** for each signer below the main status card
- Each card shows the signer's name, role, and whether they've signed yet
- The **Sign** button only appears when you're logged in with the email that matches that signer — other team members won't see it
- The investment won't be finalized until **all** signers have signed

**How signing order works with funds:**

- If your deal is set to finalize on **funds received**: both the funds and all signatures must be complete before the investment is finalized. If funds arrive before all signatures, the status shows "Awaiting signature" until the remaining signers complete their part.
- If your deal is set to finalize on **signature only**: the investment is finalized as soon as all signers have signed, regardless of when funds are received.

Once every signer has signed, Fairmint saves the completed agreement as a PDF and emails a copy to all parties.

## Understanding Your Investment Totals

The dollar amounts on an investment can be confusing because different fields mean different things depending on your deal structure:

### What's included in the investment amount

The **Investment amount** field shows the amount going toward equity. It does **not** include token warrant prices or processing fees — those appear as separate line items:

| Field | What It Includes |
|-------|-----------------|
| **Investment amount** | The amount applied toward equity (e.g., SAFE or share purchase) |
| **Token warrant** | The additional price for a token warrant (if your round includes one) |
| **Processing fees** | Any fees charged for the transaction |
| **Total to transfer** | The full amount the investor needs to send (investment + warrant + fees) |

### How token warrants affect the total

The way the warrant price is calculated depends on your invitation link type:

- **Fixed-amount links**: the warrant price is included in the fixed total — it's subtracted from the investment amount. For example, a $10,000 fixed link with a $1,000 warrant means $9,000 goes to equity and $1,000 to the warrant.
- **Variable-amount links**: the warrant price is added on top of the investment amount. For example, a $10,000 investment with a $1,000 warrant means the investor sends $11,000 total.

### Series cap vs. link maximum

You may see different error messages depending on whether the series or the invitation link is limiting the investment:

- **"Only $X remaining in this round"** — the series fundraising cap is almost reached
- **"Maximum accepted is $X"** — the invitation link has a maximum limit
