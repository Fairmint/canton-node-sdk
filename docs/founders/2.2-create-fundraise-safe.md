# Create a Fundraise (SAFE)

*This article is for founders and admins using Fairmint Studio.*

This article walks you through creating a SAFE (Simple Agreement for Future Equity) fundraising round in Fairmint. For a high-level overview of how fundraising works on the platform, see [Fundraising with Fairmint](2.0-fundraising-with-fairmint.md).

## Start a New Fundraise

1. Click **+ New** in the left menu
2. Select **Raise capital**
3. If you see a Fairmint One upgrade prompt, click **Continue** to proceed

## Set the Terms

You'll see the **"Set the terms of your fundraising round"** form. Each field has a detailed explanation on the right side of the screen — click into any field to see it.

Fill in the following fields:

---

### 1. Do you want this series to roll up investors into one line?

Choose whether to consolidate all investors from this series into a single cap table entry.

- **Yes, I want all investors of this Series into one line on my cap table.** — All investors are grouped under one entry managed by Fairmint's transfer agent.
- **No, I want each investor to appear separately on my cap table.** — Each investor gets their own line.

> **Can I roll investors up into one line on my cap table?**
>
> Fairmint offers the opportunity to roll up investors into one line on the cap table. Very convenient for founders willing to raise from VCs as well as from a larger community of angels, friends and family or users, this option guarantees an always clean cap table.
>
> This option is active for [One members](https://www.fairmint.com/fairmint-one) only. It relies on the services of Fair FP LLC, dba Fairmint TA, an SEC registered transfer agent.
>
> You can get access to [Fairmint One](https://www.fairmint.com/fairmint-one) and start rolling up investors for a one-time fee. Learn more about Fairmint One [here](https://www.fairmint.com/fairmint-one).

---

### 2. Series name

Give your fundraising round a descriptive name.

> **What is a fundraising round?**
>
> A fundraising round refers to a specific set of terms that you offer to investors. Each round, often named as Series Seed, Series A, Series B, etc., represents a different stage of company growth and valuation. The term 'Series' refers to the long-term record of securities equity ownership of a company. It is used to track stockholders and their ownership stake. In a fundraising process, Series name can be:
>
> - Series Pre-Seed SAFE I, II, III, IV...
> - Series Seed Note I, II,...
> - Series A, B, C, D
>
> A round can be reserved to specific investors only (private) or marketed online (public). SAFE and notes under the same terms will automatically be rolled-up under the same fundraising round.

---

### 3. How much do you want to raise?

Enter the target amount in dollars.

> **Amount to fundraise**
>
> Enter the target amount you aim to raise in this fundraising round. Investors will see this as the maximum fundraising goal. You can adjust this amount later if needed, it's not set in stone.

---

### 4. Do you want to solicit and advertise your fundraising?

This determines whether your fundraise is public or private, and which SEC regulation applies.

- **Yes, I want to solicit and advertise my fundraising** — allows public marketing (506(c))
- **No, I want to make a private fundraising** — limited to existing relationships (506(b))

> **What is General solicitation — Rule 506(c)?**
>
> Rule 506(c) permits you to broadly solicit and generally advertise an offering, provided that:
> - all purchasers in the offering are accredited investors
> - the issuer takes reasonable steps to verify purchasers' accredited investor status and
> - certain other conditions in Regulation D are satisfied
>
> [Read more on SEC.gov](https://www.sec.gov/education/smallbusiness/exemptofferings/rule506c)

> **What is Private placements — Rule 506(b)?**
>
> Rule 506(b) of Regulation D is considered a "safe harbor" under Section 4(a)(2). It provides objective standards that a company can rely on to meet the requirements of the Section 4(a)(2) exemption. Companies conducting an offering under Rule 506(b) can raise an unlimited amount of money and can sell securities to an unlimited number of accredited investors.
>
> [Read more on SEC.gov](https://www.sec.gov/education/smallbusiness/exemptofferings/rule506b)

---

### 5. Instrument

Select your SAFE type from the dropdown. **You can't edit the instrument once the series is created.**

Available templates include:

- Safe: Discount, no Valuation Cap
- Safe: Discount, no Valuation Cap + Token Warrant
- Safe: MFN, no Valuation Cap, no Discount
- Safe: Valuation Cap, no Discount
- Safe: Valuation Cap, no Discount + Token Warrant
- I have my own legal agreement

Each built-in SAFE comes with pre-built Reg D and Reg S legal agreements you can preview by clicking on the instrument name.

When you select **"I have my own legal agreement"**, additional fields appear:

#### Upload PDF documents

Upload up to 10 PDF files containing your custom legal agreement(s). You can drag and drop to reorder them.

#### Type of financial instrument

> Select the underlying financial instrument that your legal agreement represents:
>
> **Convertible** – A contract that converts into equity under specific conditions, such as at a future funding round or liquidity event. This includes SAFEs, convertible notes, and similar deferred equity agreements. If your legal agreement is structured as a convertible instrument, choose Convertible.
>
> **Share** – Direct equity ownership in the company. Typically used in a priced round (e.g., Series A) or for employee compensation (e.g., stock options, RSUs). If your agreement grants the right to receive or purchase shares (e.g., a stock option agreement or an equity warrant), choose Share.

If you choose **Convertible**, three optional toggles appear:

- **Valuation cap** — toggle on and enter a dollar amount (minimum $10,000)
- **Discount** — toggle on and enter a percentage (0.1% – 99.99%). Note: the Discount is the percentage reduction applied to the next round's valuation, while the Discount Rate is the complement percentage used to calculate it (e.g., a 20% discount equals an 80% discount rate).
- **Token Warrant** — toggle on and enter a purchase price in dollars

If you choose **Share**, a **Share Price** field appears where you enter the price per share.

#### Is your legal agreement already signed by the company?

> Choose the option that best describes the current status of your agreement:
>
> **Yes, the company has pre-signed the agreement** – The agreement has already been signed by the company (e.g., the CEO or an authorized representative), and only the investor's signature is missing. This allows for immediate execution upon investor acceptance.
>
> **No, the company needs to sign each agreement** – The company has not yet signed. The CEO (or an authorized representative) will counter-sign after the investor has signed.

---

### 6. Additional terms

Depending on the instrument you selected, additional fields may appear for:

- **Valuation cap** — the maximum company valuation at which the SAFE converts
- **Discount rate** — the discount applied when converting to equity
- **Pro rata rights** — whether investors can participate in future rounds
- **Investor proxy agreement** — whether investors delegate voting rights
- **Token warrant** — with purchase price

---

### 7. Do you want to upload a pitch deck?

- **Yes, I want to upload a PDF with my pitch deck** — upload your pitch deck (max 50MB PDF) to display on your deal page
- **No, I do not need a pitch deck displayed on my deal page** — skip this step

> **What is a pitch deck?**
>
> A winning pitch deck is a crucial component of any company's fundraising efforts. It is a well-designed and persuasive presentation that has a clear and compelling story, a clever structure, and the ability to showcase the potential of your company to investors. A pitch deck is essentially a visual representation of your business plan, highlighting key aspects such as your target market, competition, and financial projections.

---

Click **Save and continue** when done.

## After Creation

Your fundraise starts in setup mode and isn't visible to investors yet. Depending on your settings:

- **If you chose a custom deal page (506(b) with customization)**: you'll be taken to the **deal page editor** to design your investor-facing page.
- **Otherwise**: you'll be taken to the **series detail page** where you can preview, set up invitation links, and publish when ready.

Before publishing, you can:
- Customize your deal page — see [Deal Page Design](2.10-deal-page-design.md)
- Set investment limits and create invitation links — see [Invite an Investor](6.2-deal-invitation-links.md)
- Configure payment methods — see [Receive Funds](9.2-bank-details-funding-options.md)
- Publish your round — see [How to Publish a Fundraise](2.3-publish-fundraise.md)

Once live, you can manage your round from the series detail page — see [Manage Your Series](2.11-manage-series-settings.md).
