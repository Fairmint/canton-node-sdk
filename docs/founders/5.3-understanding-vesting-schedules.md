# Understanding Vesting Schedules

*This article is for founders and admins using Fairmint Studio.*

Vesting determines when a grant recipient actually earns their equity. Instead of receiving everything at once, equity is released gradually over time — giving both the company and the recipient an incentive to maintain the relationship.

This article explains how vesting works in Fairmint, the available schedule options, and what each phase looks like.

---

## Key Concepts

### Cliff Period

A cliff is a waiting period at the start of a vesting schedule. No equity vests during the cliff. Once the cliff ends, a portion vests all at once.

For example, with a **1-year cliff** on a 4-year schedule, nothing vests during the first 12 months. On the 1-year anniversary, 25% vests immediately. After that, equity vests monthly.

### Monthly Vesting

After the cliff (if any), the remaining equity vests in equal monthly installments. For example, on a 4-year schedule with a 1-year cliff, the remaining 75% vests over 36 months — roughly 2.08% per month.

### No Vesting

If you choose "No Vesting," the recipient owns the full grant immediately after signing the agreement.

---

## Available Schedules

When you create a grant series, you choose one of these vesting schedules:

| Schedule | Total Duration | Cliff | How It Vests |
|----------|---------------|-------|-------------|
| **Four Years + 1 Year Cliff Period** | 4 years | 1 year | 25% at the 1-year mark, then monthly over the remaining 3 years |
| **Four Years** | 4 years | None | Equal monthly installments over 48 months |
| **Three Years** | 3 years | None | Equal monthly installments over 36 months |
| **One Year + 1 Month Cliff Period** | 1 year | 1 month | A portion vests after 1 month, then monthly over the remaining 11 months |
| **2 months w/ 1 month cliff period** | 2 months | 1 month | Half vests after 1 month, the rest vests after the second month |
| **No Vesting** | Immediate | None | 100% vests on signing |

The most common schedule for employees is **Four Years + 1 Year Cliff Period**.

## Grant Lifecycle by Phase

Here's what happens at each stage of a vesting grant, from your perspective as the company:

### 1. Pending Signature

The recipient has been invited but hasn't signed the compensation agreement yet. No vesting has started.

### 2. Pending Tax

The recipient needs to complete identity verification and/or sign a tax form (W-9 or W-8BEN) before the grant can proceed.

### 3. Cliff Period

The recipient has signed, and the clock is ticking — but no equity has vested yet. The grant detail page shows:

- **Cliff period** — duration and end date (e.g., "12 months, will end on March 15, 2027")
- **Vesting period** — total duration and end date
- **Vesting progress** — a circular indicator showing 0%

### 4. Vesting

The cliff has ended, and equity is vesting monthly. On the grant detail page you'll see:

- **Vesting progress** — a circular indicator that fills as equity vests (e.g., "42%"). Hover over it to see the exact number of units vested out of the total (e.g., "4,200 of 10,000 units are vested")
- **Last Updated** — the date of the most recent vesting calculation

### 5. Vested

All equity has vested. The circular indicator shows 100%.

What happens next depends on the grant type:

- **RSUs** — the grant is complete. If your series issues tokens, the status changes to "Ready to Issue" and you can issue them.
- **Options** — the status changes to "Ready to Exercise." The recipient can choose when to exercise their options.

### 6. Exercised (Options only)

The recipient has exercised their options, converting them into actual shares. The grant detail page shows the exercise details and links to the resulting investment record.

## Stopping a Grant Early

If a recipient leaves or the grant needs to end early, you can **stop** a grant that's currently vesting:

1. Open the grant detail page
2. Click **Stop grant** on the status card
3. Choose the **stop date** — this determines how much equity the recipient keeps
4. Review the vested amount shown in the dialog (e.g., "Mega Investor will have vested 0 out of the 100 RSU that were granted")
5. Click **Stop the grant** to confirm

Stopping a grant:
- **Preserves** all equity that has already vested through the chosen date
- **Cancels** all future vesting — no additional equity will vest after the stop date
- **Cannot be undone** — make sure the date is correct before confirming

After stopping, the grant moves to **Vested** (for RSUs) or **Ready to Exercise** (for options), reflecting only the portion that vested before the stop date.

> **Note:** "Stop" is different from "Cancel." Cancel is available only during Pending Signature, Pending Tax, or Cliff Period — before any equity has vested. It removes the grant entirely.

---

## Vesting on the Grants Table

On the grant series detail page, the Grants tab shows two circular progress indicators for each grant:

- **Vested** — how much of the grant has vested (e.g., a full blue circle for 100%)
- **Exercised** — how much has been exercised (options only)

These give you a quick at-a-glance view across all your grants without opening each one.
