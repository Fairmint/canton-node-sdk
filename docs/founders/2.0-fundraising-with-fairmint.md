# Fundraising with Fairmint

*This article is for founders and admins using Fairmint Studio.*

Fairmint gives you everything you need to run a compliant fundraising round — from legal agreements and investor onboarding to payment collection and cap table management — all from one platform.

Instead of coordinating between lawyers, transfer agents, KYC providers, and spreadsheets, you set up your round in Fairmint, share a link, and investors sign, verify, and pay in one flow.

---

## What Fairmint Handles for You

| What | How |
|------|-----|
| **Legal agreements** | Choose from built-in SAFE templates (based on Y Combinator's standard) or upload your own custom legal documents. Agreements are signed digitally by investors during the investment flow. |
| **Investor identity & compliance** | Automated KYC/KYB verification, accreditation checks (for 506(c) offerings), and AML screening — all built into the investor sign-up flow. |
| **Deal page** | A hosted investment page on your own custom domain where investors can review terms, sign agreements, and submit payment. |
| **Payment collection** | Accept bank transfers (domestic, international, SEPA) and digital currency (USDC on Ethereum, USDC/USDT/PYUSD on Solana, Canton Coin). |
| **Cap table updates** | Investments automatically appear on your cap table. If you use Roll Up, all investors consolidate into a single line. |
| **SEC filings** | Built-in Form D filing support through a partner filing agent, plus state blue sky fee tracking. |
| **Transfer restrictions** | Digital assets are programmed with lockup periods matching regulatory holding periods, and holder limits are enforced automatically. |

## How a Fundraise Works, End to End

Here's the typical flow from setup to close:

**1. Create a series** — Click **+ New > Raise capital** and define your round: name, target amount, instrument type, and terms. → [Create a New Series](2.1-create-fundraise-rollup.md) / [Set Up a SAFE](2.2-create-fundraise-safe.md)

**2. Set up your deal page** — Customize the investor-facing page with your branding, pitch deck, and offering details. → [Deal Page Design](2.10-deal-page-design.md)

**3. Configure payment methods** — Add your bank details and/or crypto wallet addresses so investors know where to send funds. → [Receive Funds](9.2-bank-details-funding-options.md)

**4. Create invitation links** — Generate shareable links (with optional passcodes) to send to prospective investors. → [Invite an Investor](6.2-deal-invitation-links.md)

**5. Publish** — When ready, click Publish to make your round live. Fairmint's team reviews your setup before it goes live. → [How to Publish a Fundraise](2.3-publish-fundraise.md)

**6. Investors sign up, verify, and invest** — Investors visit your deal page, complete KYC/KYB, sign the legal agreement, and submit payment — all in one flow.

**7. Confirm funds and issue securities** — As funds arrive, you confirm receipt in Fairmint. Digital assets representing the investment are issued automatically.

**8. Manage and monitor** — Track investments, stakeholders, and activity from your series detail page. Pause, resume, or close the round when ready. → [Manage Your Series](2.11-manage-series-settings.md)

## Key Concepts

### What is a "Series"?

A series is a fundraising round in Fairmint. Each series has its own terms, legal agreement, deal page, and invitation links. You can run multiple series simultaneously — for example, a SAFE round for angels and a separate equity round for institutional investors.

### Instruments

Fairmint provides built-in legal templates for common SAFE structures. See [What Fundraising Templates are Available?](2.9-what-fundraising-templates-are-available.md) for the full list of available templates.

For equity rounds (priced rounds with shares), you set a price per share directly.

### Roll Up

With **Roll Up**, all investors from a series consolidate into a single cap table entry — keeping your cap table clean. See [What is a Roll Up Investment?](2.7-what-is-a-roll-up-investment.md) for details.

### 506(b) vs 506(c)

When creating a series, you choose whether to allow general solicitation:

- **506(b)** (no general solicitation) — you can only share the deal with people you already know. Up to 35 non-accredited but sophisticated investors allowed. No public advertising.
- **506(c)** (general solicitation allowed) — you can advertise publicly, but all investors must be accredited and their status must be verified.

→ [Soliciting & Advertising — 506(b) vs 506(c)](2.15-soliciting-advertising-506b-vs-506c.md)

### Free Plan vs Fairmint One

- **Free plan** — 506(b) only, standard SAFE templates, basic deal page. Great for getting started.
- **Fairmint One** — adds 506(c) support, custom legal documents, Roll Up, customizable deal pages, and full cap table management. One-time $5,000 fee, lifetime access.

→ [What is Fairmint One?](1.2-what-is-fairmint-one.md)
