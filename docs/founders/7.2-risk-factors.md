# Risk Factors

*This article is for founders and admins using Fairmint Studio.*

The Risk Factors page lets you customize the risk disclosures shown on your deal page. Go to **Tax & Compliance > Risk Factors** in the left menu.

## Why Risk Factors Matter

If your offering uses general solicitation (Rule 506(c)), displaying risk factors is mandatory. Even for 506(b) offerings, listing risks helps protect you legally by ensuring investors are properly informed.

> **Work with your attorney.** The default text and categories are examples only. You should customize them with your lawyer to reflect your company's specific risks and industry.

## How the Page Is Organized

The page has two sections:

### Disclosures (Header Text)

At the top, a **"Disclosures"** card contains the introductory text that appears above your risk factor categories on the deal page. This is typically a legal preamble that frames the risks listed below.

- Edit the text in the text box
- Click **Save** to update

### Risk Factor Categories

Below the disclosures, each risk category appears as a collapsible group (e.g., "Risks related to our business", "Risks related to the offering"). Click a category to expand or collapse it. Each category contains:

- **Category name** — the heading for this group of risks
- **Risk factors** — individual risk statements that will appear on your deal page. Each risk has a checkbox — only checked risks are displayed to investors
- **Check all / Uncheck all** — quickly toggle all risks in a category
- **Add new risk** button — add your own custom risk factors (up to 15 at a time per category)
- **Save** button — save your changes

Custom categories and custom risk factors can be deleted using the trash icon. Default (non-custom) risk factors can be hidden by unchecking them but not deleted.

### Adding a New Category

Click **"Add a new category of risks"** at the bottom to create a custom category. Enter a category name and at least one risk factor, then click **Save**.

## More Information

On the right side of the page, you'll find a helpful overview that explains:

- What risk factors are and why they're required
- How anti-fraud provisions of federal securities laws apply
- A reminder to work with your attorney to customize disclosures
