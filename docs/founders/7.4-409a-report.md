# 409A Report

*This article is for founders and admins using Fairmint Studio.*

The 409A Report page helps you order a 409A valuation for your company. Go to **Tax & Compliance > 409A Report** in the left menu.

## What Is a 409A Valuation?

A 409A valuation is an independent appraisal of your company's fair market value (FMV). It's required if you issue stock options to employees, contractors, or advisors — the IRS requires that options be granted at or above FMV to avoid tax penalties under Section 409A of the Internal Revenue Code.

## How It Works

The page shows six pricing tiers based on your company's stage of development. Select the stage that best matches your company:

| Stage | Price | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| **First Stage** | $1,290 | No product revenue yet; limited expense history; incomplete management team with an idea, plan, and possibly some initial product development |
| **Second Stage** | $1,990 | No product revenue but substantive expense history; product development is underway and business challenges are becoming understood |
| **Third Stage** | $2,990 | Significant product development progress; key milestones met (e.g., hiring management team); development near completion but generally no product revenue yet |
| **Fourth Stage** | $4,490 | Met additional development milestones (e.g., first customer orders or first revenue shipments); some product revenue but still operating at a loss |
| **Fifth Stage** | Contact us | Product revenue and recently achieved breakthrough measures of financial success (breakeven or positive cash flows) |
| **Sixth Stage** | Contact us | Established financial history of profitable operations or generation of positive cash flows |

### Which option should I select?

The stages follow the AICPA Valuation Practice Aid, which defines six stages of enterprise development. Read the descriptions on the page to find the one that best matches where your company is today.

For First through Fourth Stage, clicking a card opens a Stripe checkout page where you can pay by credit/debit card. For Fifth and Sixth Stage, clicking **"Contact us"** opens a pre-filled email to the Fairmint team for a custom quote.

## When You Need a 409A

- **Before granting stock options** — you need a current 409A valuation to set the exercise price
- **Every 12 months** — valuations expire after one year, so you'll need a new one annually
- **After a material event** — a new fundraising round, major revenue change, or other event that significantly affects company value
