# Understanding SAFEs

*This article is for founders and admins using Fairmint Studio.*

A **SAFE** (Simple Agreement for Future Equity) is a convertible instrument that gives investors the right to receive equity in the future, typically when a priced round occurs. SAFEs are the most common fundraising instrument for early-stage startups. In Fairmint, a SAFE series handles the full lifecycle — from creating the offering to collecting signatures, receiving funds, and tracking conversions.

## When to Use SAFEs

Use a SAFE series when:

- You are raising a **pre-seed or seed round**
- You want a **simple, founder-friendly** instrument without interest rates or maturity dates
- Your legal agreement is structured as a **convertible** instrument (SAFE, convertible note, or similar deferred equity agreement)
- You want Fairmint to handle **KYC, payments, and document signing** for you

If you are conducting a priced round with a set share price, use a [Shares series](4.1-understanding-shares.md) instead.

## Key SAFE Concepts

### Valuation Cap

The maximum company valuation at which the SAFE converts into equity. A lower cap gives the investor a better price per share when conversion happens. This protects early investors from excessive dilution.

### Discount Rate

The percentage discount the SAFE holder receives on the price per share at conversion, compared to what new investors pay in the priced round. For example, a 20% discount means the SAFE holder pays 80% of the price new investors pay.

### Token Warrant

An optional add-on that gives the SAFE holder the right to purchase tokens if the company later issues a token. When enabled, you set a **purchase price** for the warrant.

### Conversion Trigger

SAFEs typically convert into equity when:

- A **priced equity round** occurs (e.g., Series A)
- A **liquidity event** happens (acquisition, IPO)
- A **dissolution** of the company occurs

## Creating a SAFE Series

SAFEs are created through the **Raise capital** flow:

1. Click **+ New** in the left menu
2. Select **Raise capital**
3. In the series creation form, under **Type of financial instrument**, select **Convertible (Safe, Note)**
4. Configure optional terms: **Valuation Cap**, **Discount**, and **Token Warrant**
5. Complete the remaining fields (series name, amount to raise, solicitation type, legal agreement, pitch deck)
6. Click **Save and continue**

For a detailed field-by-field walkthrough, see [Create a Fundraise (SAFE)](2.2-create-fundraise-safe.md).

## Managing a SAFE Series

Once created, the SAFE series appears under the **SAFEs** section in the left menu. Clicking it opens the Series Detail page with tabs:

- **Stakeholders** — view investors and their SAFE holdings
- **Investments** — track individual SAFE investments and their statuses
- **Invitation Links** — create and manage investor invitation links
- **Activity** — see a timeline of events (signatures, payments, etc.)
- **Compliance** — KYC/AML verification status
- **Terms** — view deal terms including valuation cap, discount, and token warrant

For details on managing your series, see [Manage Your Series](2.11-manage-series-settings.md).

## Where to Find SAFEs

- **Left menu**: Under the **SAFEs** section (appears once you create a SAFE series)
- **Cap Table**: SAFEs show up in [Summary View](3.4-cap-table-summary-view.md) under "Outstanding Convertible Securities"
- **Dashboard**: Active SAFE series appear in the Fundraising section of your [Dashboard](1.6-dashboard-overview.md)

When creating a SAFE series, you choose your solicitation type. See [Soliciting & Advertising — 506(b) vs 506(c)](2.15-soliciting-advertising-506b-vs-506c.md) for details.
