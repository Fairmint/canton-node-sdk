# Jurisdictions

*This article is for founders and admins using Fairmint Studio.*

The Jurisdictions page lets you control which countries your investors can invest from and what rules apply. Go to **Tax & Compliance > Jurisdictions** in the left menu.

The page subtitle reads: *"Set country-specific eligibility rules and exemptions for your offering."*

## Accreditation Requirements

The top card lets you decide which countries require investors to be accredited.

### What is an accredited investor?

An accredited investor is someone allowed to invest in private offerings under local laws. In the U.S., this includes individuals with $200K+ annual income (or $300K with a spouse) for the last two years, or $1M+ net worth excluding their primary residence.

### Require Accreditation Everywhere

A switch at the top lets you **"Require investors from every country to be accredited."**

- **Switch ON** — all eligible investors are treated as investing under Regulation D (U.S. exemption for accredited investors). No country-specific list needed.
- **Switch OFF** — only investors from countries you add below are treated as investing under Regulation D. Investors from all other countries are treated as investing under Regulation S (U.S. exemption for non-U.S. investors investing offshore).

### Country List

When the switch is off, you can search and add countries to the accreditation list. Added countries appear as removable labels (click the X to remove one).

**Permanent entries:**
- **United States** is always in the accreditation list and cannot be removed

**Conflict check:** A country cannot be in both the accredited list and the prohibited list. If you try to add a country that's already in the other list, you'll see an error message.

Click **Save Changes** when done. You'll be asked to confirm before saving.

## Prohibited Countries

The bottom card lets you block investors from specific countries entirely. Investors from prohibited countries will be fully restricted from participating in your offering.

Search and add countries the same way as the accreditation section. Prohibited countries appear as red labels.

**Permanent entries (OFAC-sanctioned countries):**
- Cuba, Iran, North Korea, Syria, Venezuela, and Russia are always prohibited and cannot be removed

Click **Save Changes** when done.

## How Accreditation and Prohibition Interact

The two lists are mutually exclusive — a country cannot be in both. Here's how they work together:

| Country Status | What Happens |
|---------------|-------------|
| In **Accredited** list | Investors must prove accreditation (Regulation D applies) |
| In **Prohibited** list | Investors are blocked entirely |
| In **neither** list (switch off) | Investors can participate under Regulation S (offshore exemption) |
| Switch ON (all accredited) | Every non-prohibited country requires accreditation |
