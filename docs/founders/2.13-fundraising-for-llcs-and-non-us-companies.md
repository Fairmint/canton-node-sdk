# Fundraising for LLCs & Non-US Companies

*This article is for founders and admins using Fairmint Studio.*

## For LLCs

If you're operating a Limited Liability Company (LLC) and looking to raise funds through Fairmint, this guide is tailored to help you understand the specific requirements and considerations for LLCs in the fundraising process.

### Essential Requirements

- **Custom Legal Documents Required**: Since Fairmint provides pre-made legal templates exclusively for C-Corporations, LLCs will need to supply their own legal documents. These documents should be specifically drafted to comply with the legal structure and regulations applicable to LLCs.
- **Use of Fairmint One**: To accommodate the specific needs of your LLC and the legal documents you provide, you will need to use Fairmint One. This platform offers the flexibility and customization necessary to ensure your fundraising efforts align with your LLC's legal framework and business goals.

### Consideration for Converting to a C-Corp

While LLCs can effectively use Fairmint to raise capital, it's worth considering the potential benefits of converting to a C-Corporation if extensive fundraising is part of your long-term strategy:

- **Attracting Investors**: C-Corps are often more attractive to investors, especially venture capitalists, due to familiar structure and governance features.
- **Simplified Fundraising**: With pre-made legal templates and generally more streamlined processes available for C-Corps, fundraising can be simpler and potentially more successful.

## For Non-US Companies

If your company is based outside of the US and you're considering using Fairmint for your fundraising efforts, here's some important information to help guide you through the process smoothly and ensure compliance with your local regulations.

### Essential Requirements

- **Provide Your Custom Legal Documents**: At Fairmint, we understand the unique legal environments in which non-US companies operate. As we don't provide specific legal templates for non-US jurisdictions, you'll need to supply your own legal documents that are customized to meet the laws and investment regulations of your country.
- **Utilizing Fairmint One**: For the flexibility and customization required by diverse international legal standards, non-US based companies must use Fairmint One. This platform is designed to accommodate the variety of needs that arise from different legal systems around the world.

### Considering US Incorporation

Many non-US companies consider incorporating in the United States due to certain advantages:

- **Familiar Legal Framework**: The US legal framework is well-known and often favored by global investors, potentially making it easier to attract a broader investment base.
- **Access to US Markets**: Incorporating in the US can also facilitate easier access to the US markets, which might be beneficial depending on your business objectives.
