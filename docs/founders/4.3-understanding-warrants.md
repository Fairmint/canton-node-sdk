# Understanding Warrants

*This article is for founders and admins using Fairmint Studio.*

A **warrant** is a financial instrument that gives the holder the right to purchase shares at a specific price within a set timeframe. In Fairmint, warrants are tracked as their own series type with a dedicated detail page showing warrant holders and issuance metrics.

## When to Use Warrants

Use a Warrant series when:

- You have issued **standalone warrants** alongside a SAFE or convertible note
- You need to track **token warrants** separately from the underlying SAFE
- Investors hold **rights to purchase shares** at a predetermined price in the future
- You are importing warrant records from your existing cap table

Warrants are often paired with SAFEs — when you enable the **Token Warrant** option during SAFE creation, a separate warrant series may be created to track those rights. See [Create a Fundraise (SAFE)](2.2-create-fundraise-safe.md) for details.

## How Warrants Differ from Other Instrument Types

For a comparison of all instrument types, see [Understanding Shares](4.1-understanding-shares.md#how-shares-differ-from-other-instrument-types).

## The Warrant Detail Page

When you click a warrant series in the left menu, you see the **Warrant Detail** page. This page is simpler than the standard Series Detail page and contains:

### Header

Displays the warrant series **name** (e.g., "Warrant A").

### Metrics

A single key metric card:

- **TOTAL WARRANTS ISSUED** — the count of warrant holders for this series

### Warrant Holders Table

A searchable, paginated table listing all warrant holders with columns:

- **Date** — when the warrant was issued (date and time)
- **Name** — the warrant holder's name
- **Status** — current status of the warrant (e.g., WARRANT SIGNED)

You can search by name or email, and the table supports pagination (100 rows per page by default).

## Where to Find Warrants

- **Left menu**: Under the **Warrants** section (appears once you have a warrant series)
- **Cap Table**: Warrants show up in [Summary View](3.4-cap-table-summary-view.md) under "Outstanding Convertible Securities"
- **Dashboard**: Active warrant series appear in the Fundraising section of your [Dashboard](1.6-dashboard-overview.md)
